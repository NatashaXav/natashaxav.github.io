2.1. Foundational RFM Scoring

The analysis begins with RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) scores to quantify customer behaviour. These metrics are powerful predictors of a customer's future engagement.

  • Recency (R): Days since the customer's last purchase. (Lower is better)
  • Frequency (F): Total number of transactions in the analysis period. (Higher is better)
  • Monetary (M): Total revenue generated by the customer. (Higher is better)

Customers are scored on a scale of 1-5 for each dimension based on quintiles. A customer with a score of '555' is in the top 20% for all three metrics—a "Champion".


import pandas as pd

# Assume 'df' is a DataFrame with CustomerID, InvoiceDate, and TotalPrice
snapshot_date = df['InvoiceDate'].max() + pd.DateOffset(days=1)

# Calculate RFM metrics
rfm_data = df.groupby('CustomerID').agg({
    'InvoiceDate': lambda date: (snapshot_date - date.max()).days,
    'InvoiceNo': 'count',
    'TotalPrice': 'sum'
})
rfm_data.rename(columns={'InvoiceDate': 'Recency',
                         'InvoiceNo': 'Frequency',
                         'TotalPrice': 'Monetary'}, inplace=True)

# Calculate RFM scores (quintiles)
rfm_data['R_Score'] = pd.qcut(rfm_data['Recency'], 5, labels=[5, 4, 3, 2, 1])
rfm_data['F_Score'] = pd.qcut(rfm_data['Frequency'].rank(method='first'), 5, labels=[1, 2, 3, 4, 5])
rfm_data['M_Score'] = pd.qcut(rfm_data['Monetary'], 5, labels=[1, 2, 3, 4, 5])

rfm_data['RFM_Score'] = rfm_data.R_Score.astype(str) + rfm_data.F_Score.astype(str) + rfm_data.M_Score.astype(str)

2.3. Predictive Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) Modeling

To forecast future revenue, a predictive CLV model was implemented. This moves beyond historical value to estimate the future worth of a customer. A common approach is using the BG/NBD (Beta-Geometric/Negative Binomial Distribution) model for transaction prediction and the Gamma-Gamma model for monetary value prediction.

A simplified predictive CLV can be calculated as:

CLV = (Average Order Value * Purchase Frequency) * Customer Lifetime

Where Customer Lifetime = 1 / Churn Rate. By calculating this at a segment level, we can make strategic decisions about how much to invest in acquisition and retention.

2.4. Cohort and Transition Analysis

  • Cohort Analysis: Customers were grouped by their acquisition month to track their behaviour and retention over time. This helps identify if recent customer cohorts are more or less valuable than older ones.
  • Transition Matrix: A matrix was created to show how customers migrate between segments over time (e.g., from "Potential Loyalist" to "Loyal Customer", or from "Loyal Customer" to "At Risk"). This is crucial for measuring the effectiveness of retention and growth strategies.

4. UK Retail Applications: Segment-Specific Strategies

The true power of segmentation lies in its application. The following table outlines tailored marketing strategies for each segment, contextualised for the UK market.

Segment Profile UK-Focused Marketing Strategy
Champions Bought recently, buy often, and spend the most. Your VIPs. Nurture & Reward: Exclusive access to new products, invitations to special events (e.g., a private shopping night at a London flagship store), personal shopper services, premium loyalty tier with enhanced benefits.
Loyal Customers Buy on a regular basis. Responsive to promotions. Upsell & Engage: Personalised cross-sell recommendations based on purchase history. Offer early access to sales events (e.g., Boxing Day sales). Build a community around their interests.
Potential Loyalists Recent customers with average frequency. High potential. Incentivise & Onboard: Offer a loyalty program incentive (e.g., "Make one more purchase to unlock free delivery for a year"). Targeted onboarding content showcasing product value.
At Risk Bought some time ago, but used to be frequent and valuable. Re-engage Proactively: Automated, personalised "We miss you" campaigns with a compelling, time-sensitive offer (e.g., a voucher for a popular UK brand like M&S or John Lewis). Solicit feedback to understand reasons for lapsing.
New Customers First-time buyers. Impress & Guide: A best-in-class welcome series. Post-purchase support and content that reinforces their decision. A small, surprise incentive for their second purchase.
Lost Customers Lowest recency, frequency, and monetary scores. Win-Back (Low-Effort): Include in major seasonal campaigns (e.g., Black Friday, Christmas) with a strong "come back" offer. If unresponsive after a period, remove from active mailing lists to improve efficiency.
